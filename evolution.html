Artificial Intelligence: ChatGPT's Evolution and Its Far-Reaching Effects

Published on: January 5, 2025 | Author: Jacob Daniel Rodriguez

In November 2022, the release of ChatGPT changed how people find, use, and share information. It quickly gained attention for its advanced abilities, surprising many users. ChatGPT is the result of years of work on chatbots and programs that mimic human conversations, both written and spoken.

The acronym GPT in ChatGPT stands for Generative Pre-trained Transformer. The Transformer model enhances the chatbot's ability not just to predict the next word but to generate coherent paragraphs. While remarkable, these systems are not infallible, as their outputs are not always fact-checked. This discussion focuses on ChatGPT as a specific artificial intelligence (AI) program while using the broader term AI to refer to the expanding array of such technologies.

The Impact

ChatGPT's impact has sparked debates, with both positive and negative perspectives emerging. Some academics perceive these tools as threats to comprehensive learning, viewing them as shortcuts that hinder the development of critical thinking and originality. Conversely, other educators embrace ChatGPT for its potential to facilitate brainstorming, overcome writer’s block, and produce initial skills essential in a world increasingly shaped by such technologies. Crucially, ignoring these developments is no longer an option for academic.

Potential

As with any innovation, both benefits and challenges are offered by ChatGPT. The duality of this technology is highlighted by Wu (2023) in the report, “Professors Published a Paper on AI with a ‘Plot Twist’—ChatGPT Wrote It.” A paper that initially passed peer review was later revealed to have been generated by ChatGPT, and errors were subsequently identified by reviewers. Similarly, it was reported by Paul (2023) that academic abstracts generated by ChatGPT passed peer review 32% of the time, even when reviewers were informed that some abstracts were AI-generated.

Unlike traditional proofreading tools that address spelling, grammar, and citation errors, complete texts are produced by ChatGPT. It was found by Bushard (2023) that scientists were deceived by ChatGPT nearly one-third of the time. However, a countermeasure against potential misuse has been offered by AI detection tools, such as GPT-2 Output Detector and Turnitin, which have adapted by incorporating AI detection features.

Applications

Developing critical reading and editing skills among students is a significant but necessary challenge. Embracing this shift is essential in promoting digital literacy and academic integrity in an era dominated by AI tools like ChatGPT.

Positive Uses of ChatGPT:

Brainstorming: Helps students generate ideas and overcome writer’s block.

Writing Assistance: Produces initial drafts and proofreads for spelling, grammar, and citation accuracy.

Research Support: Aids in locating relevant sources and summarizing information.

Digital Literacy: Encourages evaluating the reliability of information and cross-referencing with credible sources.

Academic Integrity: Detects instances of plagiarism and promotes critical thinking and originality.

Iterative Responses: The “Regenerate Response” feature allows users to refine outputs, fostering more nuanced conversations.

Negative Aspects of ChatGPT:

Reduced Critical Thinking: Over-reliance may diminish originality and problem-solving skills.

Potential for Misuse: Can be exploited to create fraudulent academic content.

Inaccuracy: May produce unreliable or erroneous information.

Overdependence on AI: Risks replacing human creativity and engagement with technology.

Erosion of Skills: Excessive reliance on AI tools could weaken essential human abilities.

Conclusion

ChatGPT, powered by Large Language Models, is a transformative tool designed to simulate and process human interactions. While its potential for brainstorming, drafting, and research is undeniable, users must exercise caution to mitigate its drawbacks, such as inaccuracies and overreliance. By fostering critical thinking, digital literacy, and responsible use, society can harness ChatGPT's benefits while addressing its challenges. As ChatGPT itself warns, "ChatGPT may produce inaccurate information about people, places, or facts." Verification remains a fundamental step in utilizing this technology effectively.
